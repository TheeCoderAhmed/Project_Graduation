[Normal] Abstract
[Normal] Information asymmetry regarding service quality remains a common issue in the healthcare sector, leading to poor patient experiences, increased waiting times, and limited provider accountability. Most traditional healthcare information systems focus mainly on clinical data management and do not sufficiently consider patient satisfaction or experiential feedback. To address this problem, this project proposes a patient-centered evaluation and feedback platform that improves transparency in the selection of healthcare providers.
[Normal] The proposed system is developed using Flutter for the mobile application interface and Firebase for authentication and real-time database management. It enables users to compare doctors and pharmacies through structured reviews, star ratings, and post-visit questionnaires stored in a centralized mobile platform. The main objective is to reduce the effect of unverified opinions and improve decision-making by presenting more reliable provider rankings. Unlike integrated hospital information systems, DRAPO is designed as an independent standalone platform focused entirely on patient-centered healthcare service evaluation.
[Normal] The project also includes the design and implementation of a secure mobile environment with authenticated user access and protected cloud data storage. To evaluate providers fairly, the system applies a weighted provider evaluation approach based on structured patient feedback and predefined service quality criteria.
[Normal] These methods assign weights to important criteria such as communication skills, waiting time, cleanliness, empathy, and service efficiency. For example, communication quality may receive a higher weight than waiting time depending on expert evaluation.
[Normal] To improve realism and fairness, feedback from healthcare professionals such as doctors and pharmacists will be considered during questionnaire design and criteria weighting. The expected outcome of the project is to provide useful data-driven guidance for patients while also helping healthcare providers understand and improve patient satisfaction. Keywords: Healthcare Evaluation, Patient Experience, Flutter, Firebase, AHP, Healthcare Ranking System

[Normal] This information gap can lead to poor healthcare decisions, dissatisfaction, and reduced trust in service providers. At the same time, doctors, pharmacies, and clinic administrators may not receive organized feedback that can help them identify weaknesses and improve their services.

The platform aims to transform individual experiences into useful and measurable insights, helping patients make better choices while encouraging providers to improve service quality.
[Heading 2] 1.2 Scope
[Normal] DRAPO is a patient-centered healthcare evaluation platform designed to improve transparency in healthcare service selection through structured patient feedback and data-driven provider rankings. The system is developed as a mobile application where users can evaluate healthcare providers such as doctors, pharmacies, and clinics based on real patient service experiences.
[Normal] The project focuses on non-clinical service quality factors that directly affect patient satisfaction. Instead of managing medical records or treatment processes, the platform emphasizes measurable experience criteria such as waiting time, communication quality, professionalism, and cleanliness.
[Normal] The key aspects of the project include:
[Normal] • User Authentication: Secure user registration, login, password recovery, and profile management using Firebase Authentication. This ensures that only authorized users can access personal accounts and submit trusted feedback.
[Normal] • Provider Search and Categories: Users can search for doctors, pharmacies, and clinics by name, specialty, or location. The platform also provides categorized browsing options such as doctors, pharmacies, and nearby providers for easier navigation.
[Normal] • Structured Evaluation System: After receiving a healthcare service, users can complete post-visit questionnaires and star ratings based on criteria such as waiting time, Waiting Time, Service Quality, Hygiene, and Staff Communication. This transforms experiences into measurable service data.
[Normal] • Anonymous Reviews: Users may submit feedback anonymously if they prefer not to reveal their identity publicly. This encourages more honest evaluations while maintaining privacy.
[Normal] • Weighted Ranking Algorithm: Provider rankings are calculated using weighted criteria rather than simple average ratings. Important factors such as Staff Communication or Hygiene can have greater influence on the final score, producing more realistic rankings.
[Normal] • Provider Profiles: Each healthcare provider has a profile page containing name, specialization, rating score, review count, and service quality indicators. This allows patients to compare providers before making decisions.
[Normal] • Analytics Dashboard: Healthcare providers or administrators can monitor rating trends, total reviews, score distributions, strengths, and improvement areas through visual dashboards and summaries.
[Normal] • Cloud Backend Infrastructure: Firebase Firestore is used to securely manage provider data, user reviews, provider rankings, questionnaires, and analytics data. Real-time synchronization ensures that updates are reflected immediately across the platform.
[Normal] • Privacy Protection: The system is designed to protect users through secure authentication and controlled data storage. No confidential medical records, diagnoses, prescriptions, or treatment histories are stored in the platform.
[Normal] • Scalability and Adaptability: Although the first version may operate with a limited provider database, the platform architecture supports future expansion to more cities, additional healthcare categories, and increased user traffic without redesigning the core system.
[Normal] • Societal Impact: By reducing information asymmetry in healthcare provider selection, the platform can help patients make better decisions, encourage competition based on service quality, and improve public trust in healthcare services.
[Normal] DRAPO does not aim to replace full-scale hospital management systems, prescription systems, or official medical record platforms. It is intended as an independent decision-support and patient feedback platform that enhances healthcare service selection through transparent evaluations and healthcare provider discovery and evaluation features. While the rating model focuses on non-clinical service quality, provider categorization also includes medical specialties for easier discovery.

[Heading 2] 3.1 Introduction
[Normal] The healthcare sector has undergone significant digital transformation in recent years(WHO, 2023). Many healthcare organizations now use online systems for prescription management and patient record storage. Although these systems improve efficiency, they mainly focus on administrative and clinical functions. Patients still have limited access to structured and reliable information regarding experiential service quality, such as waiting times, communication skills, staff behavior, and overall satisfaction. As a result, many individuals rely on personal recommendations or scattered online comments when selecting healthcare providers.
[Heading 2] 3.2 Patient Satisfaction and Service Quality
[Normal] Patient satisfaction is widely recognized as an important indicator of healthcare quality. Modern studies show that healthcare success is not based only on treatment outcomes, but also on how patients are treated during the service process. Factors such as empathy, respectful communication, responsiveness, and environmental cleanliness strongly influence trust and satisfaction. Therefore, measuring experiential quality is essential for building a more transparent and patient-centered healthcare environment. (Liu & Jiang, 2021)
[Heading 2] 3.3 Limitations of Existing Review Platforms
[Normal] Healthcare users commonly rely on systems such as e-Nabız for records and appointments, while review platforms such as DoktorTakvimi and Zocdoc are used for provider selection. However, these systems usually depend on simple star ratings or unstructured text comments. Such methods may create unreliable results because users often score services according to personal emotions or inconsistent standards. In addition, fake reviews or biased comments may reduce fairness and trust. Therefore, traditional review platforms may not accurately represent the actual quality of healthcare providers.
[Heading 2] 3.4 Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP)
[Normal] The Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) is a structured decision-making technique used to determine the relative importance of evaluation criteria through pairwise comparisons and weighted priorities. AHP is used to determine the relative importance of each criterion through pairwise comparisons and weighted priorities. For example, communication quality may receive a higher weight than waiting time, while hygiene may be weighted more heavily than facility appearance. After determining these priorities, weighted provider scores can be calculated more fairly and consistently. This allows the system to reflect realistic patient expectations more accurately. (Al Awadh, 2022)
[Heading 2] 3.5 Mobile Application Technologies
[Normal] Mobile technologies play an important role in improving healthcare accessibility. Frameworks such as Flutter allow developers to build applications for both Android and iOS. This reduces development time, lowers costs, and provides a consistent user experience. Since smartphones are widely used in everyday life, a mobile-based evaluation platform makes it easier for users to submit reviews, compare providers, and access updated provider rankings conveniently.(Fentaw, 2020; Zarichuk, 2023).
[Heading 2] 3.6 Database and Real-Time Systems
[Normal] For backend infrastructure, Firebase Firestore provides a strong NoSQL database solution for dynamic mobile applications. Firebase supports secure user authentication, scalable cloud storage, and real-time synchronization. These features are highly valuable for DRAPO because provider scores, reviews, and rankings may continuously change. Real-time updates help ensure that users always receive the latest available information. (Valkamo, 2025)
[Heading 2] 3.7 Feasibility and Contribution
[Normal] Based on the reviewed literature, there is a clear need for a patient-focused platform that evaluates experiential healthcare quality. DRAPO aims to combine structured questionnaires, weighted ranking algorithms, and mobile accessibility into one integrated system. Unlike traditional rating websites, the proposed platform emphasizes fairness, transparency, and measurable evaluation criteria. Therefore, the reviewed literature supports both the necessity and practical feasibility and relevance of the DRAPO project.

[Heading 2] 7.4.1 Algorithmic Race Conditions and Concurrent Writes
[Normal] Problem: If multiple patients completed visits and uploaded reviews for the same doctor at the same time, simultaneous client-side calculations would overwrite each other, causing serious data corruption in the provider's global score metrics.
[Normal] Resolution: The team moved all mathematical operations away from the client-side app. By utilizing the serverless Firestore onDocumentWritten trigger, calculations are queued and processed securely on the backend. The function recalculates the statistics using secure database transactions and writes the output back via an atomic database merge, eliminating race conditions.
[Heading 2] 7.4.2 Unique Review ID Management
[Normal] Problem: To prevent leaderboard manipulation, the platform needed to ensure that an individual patient could only submit one review per provider encounter. However, to comply with KVKK privacy regulations, the patient's identity had to be strictly decoupled from public viewing.
[Normal] Resolution: The team implemented a deterministic composite document ID strategy within firestore.rules. When a review is created, the system forces the document ID to follow a strict structure: request.auth.uid + '_' + providerId. If a user tries to submit a second review for the same provider, the write operation maps to the exact same document path and is rejected by the database rules, enforcing the one-review limit while safely preserving patient anonymity.

[Normal] Figure B.1: Weighted Provider Evaluation Workflow
[Normal] Figure B.1 illustrates the workflow of the weighted provider evaluation mechanism used in the DRAPO platform. The process begins when a patient submits a structured healthcare service review through the mobile application. The system then collects questionnaire-based evaluation scores related to different service quality criteria such as communication quality, professionalism, cleanliness, and waiting time.
[Normal] After collecting the evaluation data, the platform applies predefined importance weights to each criterion to reflect their relative impact on overall patient satisfaction. The weighted scores are then processed to calculate a final provider evaluation score.
[Normal] Once the calculation process is completed, the updated provider score is stored in the Firebase Firestore database and reflected in the provider ranking system displayed to users. This workflow allows DRAPO to generate more realistic and balanced healthcare provider rankings instead of relying only on simple average star ratings.

[Normal] Table B.1 presents an example of the weighted evaluation criteria used in the DRAPO healthcare provider ranking system. The table illustrates how different healthcare service quality factors are assigned different importance levels during the provider evaluation process.
[Normal] The weighting strategy is designed to generate more realistic and patient-centered provider rankings instead of relying only on simple average star ratings. Criteria such as communication quality are assigned higher weights because effective communication strongly influences patient trust, understanding, and overall healthcare experience. Additional factors including cleanliness, professionalism, waiting time, and empathy are also incorporated into the evaluation model to ensure balanced healthcare service quality assessment.
[Normal] These evaluation criteria and their corresponding weights were determined based on healthcare service quality research, patient satisfaction concepts, and the overall objectives of the DRAPO platform. The weighted evaluation structure allows the system to produce more meaningful provider scores while improving fairness, transparency, and decision-making reliability for patients.
